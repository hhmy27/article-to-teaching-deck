# Deck blueprint

Use this default sequence unless the article strongly suggests a better teaching order.

## Language rule

- Default to the user's conversation language.
- Do not mirror the article language unless the user asks for that explicitly.
- Keep names like `Claude Code`, `Excalidraw`, or `Opus 4.6` in their standard product form when translating.

## Standalone teaching rule

- Write for a learner who has not read the article.
- Explain the idea directly instead of assuming article context.
- Prefer conclusion-first slides over chronology-first slides unless chronology is the lesson.

## Accessibility rule

- Prefer very short lines.
- Avoid paragraphs.
- Use more slides instead of denser slides.
- Use one key idea per slide.
- Favor simple diagrams and action steps over abstract summaries.

## Default flow

1. Title and framing
2. Why this article matters
3. Core thesis in one sentence
4. Big-picture map of the article
5. Key concept or vocabulary slide
6. Main idea 1
7. Main idea 2
8. Main idea 3
9. Timeline / process / causal chain
10. Evidence, example, or case study
11. Tension, limitation, or debate
12. Recap
13. Knowledge check
14. Extension prompts

## Slide patterns

### Orientation

- learner promise
- prior-knowledge hook
- "what you will understand by the end"

### Core explanation

- stepwise reveal
- before/after contrast
- timeline
- causal chain
- concept map
- example then principle

### Retention

- three-key-takeaway recap
- short-answer question
- application question
- "what would change if..." transfer prompt

## Footer guidance

Use short source locators in slide footers:

- `Source: Overview, para 2`
- `Source: Section heading, paras 1-2`
- `Source: Conclusion`

Do not place full quotes in footers. Keep them auditable and compact.
